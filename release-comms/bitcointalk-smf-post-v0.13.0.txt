[size=12pt][b]The Problem Nobody Talked About[/b][/size]

LWMA-3 retargets fast. ~30 blocks. That's the whole point. But [b]LWMA-3 cannot retarget at all without blocks being mined[/b]. The algorithm is a moving average — no new data point, no new average.

So picture this: AuxPoW activates at block 2,000,000. Some opportunistic LTC pool sniffs Slack, decides to merge-mine (BOB) for a few hours, drives the difficulty into orbit, and then [b]drops us[/b] to chase something fancier. The pool leaves. The lone (BOB) miner's ~135 MH/s scrypt is now staring at a difficulty that was set when there was 7,000,000× more hash on the network. Expected time to next block at that difficulty: [b]years[/b].

LWMA-3 can fix it. But LWMA-3 needs blocks to do it. No blocks → no fix. Chicken, meet egg. The Conspiracy meets its dream scenario: (BOB) silenced by its own difficulty adjustment, no 51% attack required.

[size=12pt][b]The Rule[/b][/size]

[b]v0.13.0 hardforks (BOB) at block 1,888,888 — ~2026-07-16 ~07:00 UTC — for one new consensus rule:[/b]

[list]
[li][b]Emergency-difficulty recovery.[/b] After a 6-hour stall ([tt]block.nTime - prev.nTime > 21600[/tt]), the chain will accept a single block at [b]min-difficulty[/b] regardless of what LWMA-3 computed. That block's long solvetime then enters the LWMA-3 window and difficulty starts coming back down. Normal retargeting takes over within ~N blocks. The chicken-and-egg is broken.[/li]
[/list]

The rule has teeth. [b]Both[/b] conditions must hold — strictly more than 6 hours AND the miner declaring min-difficulty. Exactly 6h on the nose doesn't qualify. And if a miner finds a block at better-than-min in the meantime, [b]they must publish it at the better target.[/b] The exemption is a safety valve, not a free pass for lazy miners. Slack is not freely given. Slack is earned by submitting the work you actually did.
